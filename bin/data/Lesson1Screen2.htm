Lesson 1 Part 2: Vowels, Hard and Soft

 

There are two types of vowels in Russian: hard and soft. A hard vowel is like a vowel in English, but a soft vowel has ‘y’ (as in yet) before the vowel sound when the letter appears at the beginning of a word or before another vowel. After a consonant, the vowel has the same sound as its hard variant, and the consonant is softened if this is possible (usually it is). Most vowels come in hard and soft pairs.

Please note that Ёё is often written as Ее in native Russian texts.

Technically, Ии and Ыы are pairs for the purposes of unsoftenable consonants (see next screen), but have entirely different sounds.

All of the vowels have their independent sound as their name (Ёё= yo), except for Йй, which is known as и краткоя (ee KRAT-kuh-ya), which means “short i”.

Please also note that ‘Ёё’ and ‘Оо’ in unstressed syllables sound like ‘Яя’ and ‘Аа’ respectively, and ‘Аа’ and ‘Яя’ in unstressed syllables sound like ‘Ээ’ and ‘Ее’ respectively.
