Lesson 2 Part 1: Numbers 0 - 10

Russian numbers have cardinal (like one, two, three) and ordinal (like first, second, third) forms. Russian has three genders: Masculine, Feminine, and Neuter; all nouns have an inherent gender, and ordinal numbers use different forms when referring to nouns of different genders.

Один is the default masculine form of the number, which agrees with the gender of its referent even in cardinal form. It is одНА in feminine and одНО in neuter.

When counting (1, 2, 3), раз is used instead of один (раз means “time”, as in “the second time”. In Russian, counting goes: “time, two, three, four..”).
