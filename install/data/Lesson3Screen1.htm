Lesson 3 Part 1: A note of Russian cases

Russian has a grammatical category called case that applies to words to indicate their use in a sentence. They are as follows:



Name

Use

Nominative

Default case, indicates subject of a sentence.

e.g. I ate cheese.

я ел сыр

Accusative

Indicates direct object of a sentence.

e.g. I ate cheese.

я ел сыр

Dative

Indicates indirect object of a sentence. Often corresponds to “to” in English.

e.g. I gave some cheese to Bob.

я дал сыр Бобу

Genitive

Indicates something to which a noun in another case belongs.

i.e. Bob’s car

машина Боба

Instrumental

Indicates that an action is done using something (like a tool).

e.g. I ate cheese with a knife.

я ел сыр ножом

Prepositional

Used on a noun that follows a preposition.

i.e. I live in a house.

я живу в доме
