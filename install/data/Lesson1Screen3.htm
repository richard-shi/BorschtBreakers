Lesson 1 Part 3: Consonants

All consonants are hard by default, and most become soft before a soft vowel or a soft sign (see next lesson). This softness is not the same as the softness of the vowel; in this case it involves moving the tongue closer to the palate. A minority of consonants cannot be softened, and thus make the soft vowel that comes after these consonants sound as though it is the corresponding hard vowel instead. The result is that these consonants never have hard vowel following them.
