Lesson 1 Part 1: The Russian Alphabet and Sounds

 

The Russian alphabet is called “Cyrillic” (in Russian “Кириллица”, pronounced K’ee-R’EE-l’ee-tsa), has 33 letters, with 20 consonants, 11 vowels, and 2 additional modifying letters.

 

The consonants are (showing uppercase and lowercase forms):

Бб Вв Гг Дд Жж Зз Кк Лл Мм Нн Пп Рр Сс Тт Фф Хх Цц Чч Шш Щщ

 

The vowels are (showing uppercase and lowercase forms):

Аa Ее Ёё Ии Йй Оо Уу Ыы Ээ Юю Яя

 

The modifying letters are (showing uppercase and lowercase forms, even though none of them can begin a word):

Ьь Ъъ

 

The English transliterations will use  ‘  to indicate a soft consonant. Both the English transliteration and the Russian text will use capital letters to indicate a stressed syllable in a word with multiple syllables (these are not used in regular Russian).
